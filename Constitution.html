Constitution of The Captain Format ©

By HobbitWhoCould#4068

Express consent for the use of this document to The Captain format is hereby granted by this document’s Author

All rights, including but not limited to: the granting and retracting of use of this document and its contents, along with any subsequent & future changes to its original content, are held solely by the above entity 

Captain is a format that focuses on transparency, accountability, and community involvement. Captain is dedicated to creating a format that is democratically run by the community, for the community. In this endeavor, Captain will ensure that all true decision making power is left in the hands of its community. This will be accomplished by the forming of a "direct democratic" system that is devoid of a conventional governance structure. In lieu of elected leaders, Captain will instead have Elected Administrators: it is the duty of these Elected Administrators to be a Voice for the Community & to facilitate the conversations of the Community. It is not the Elected Administrators’ place to make any direct decisions on the format, its rules, or its principles. Elected Administrators will adhere to the voice of the community to ensure their wishes are given a platform for all of the Captain Community to directly vote on.

As a general overview: The Captain Community will voice its concerns over the format. This can be accomplished by any appropriate means, such as: discussing the changes on Discord/Facebook/Reddit/Twitter, directly reaching out to your Elected Administrators, or through the formal submission on Captain’s website to vote on a matter. At this point, it is the responsibility of the Elected Administrators to internally vote on which issues should be moved forward to a public vote. The elected body is not voting on any changes, simply if they believe there is enough support for a given change to justify it moving forward. Should the elected body vote to move forward with any given change, it will then be put to a public vote for the greater Captain Community to vote on. This system alleviates any blame and makes controlling of the format easier. The elected body loses all blame and all praise. It is significantly easier to hold the elected body accountable for not even letting us vote for a change than it is to hold them accountable for the result of the vote. Enough public vocalization for a change will make it near impossible for the elected body to completely ignore and not move to a vote. These measures will also help protect the integrity of the format by eliminating insider-trading of the elected body. Furthermore, it helps to protect The Captain Community from any outside influence or "trolling."

I. Captain will have the need for Elected Administrators

I. Whereas, Captain will have the need for Elected Administrators (hereby referred to as EAs),

  1. To ensure that the elected body is capable of meeting the needs of the Captain Community, large enough to foster a diverse structure, yet nimble enough to assist in cultivating an ever-changing format: there will be 7 EAs at all times.
  1. Should, at any time, the elected body fall under 7 Elected Administrators, the current elected body will do their due diligence to ensure an emergency vote is called in no less than one month from the day of falling under 7 members
  1. An emergency vote is defined as: a vote which occurs at an unscheduled time, due to an EA seat finding itself prematurely vacant due to an EA stepping down, being removed from office, etc.
  1. All EAs responsibilities, barring holding an emergency vote, will be put on hold until such time that the elected body has 7 persons
  1. As the community finds it necessary for the EAs to have an elected scribe to take notes, ensure meetings are recorded, provide order during meetings and enforce Robert's Rules,
  1. A scribe will be elected on odd-numbered years, in the same year that only 3 EAs are voted in
  1. For the Founding Year of Captain, the Scribe will be elected in 2020, to help set a pattern for precedence
  2. The initial Scribe, who shall be voted in during the Founding year, will serve only 1 year, to match pattern precedence detailed in I.C2
  1. As this is a non-voting position which requires a specific skill set and understanding of this document, this initial 1 year period will not count towards the 2 term limit & will be exempt from the restriction against consecutive terms
  1. The Election of a Scribe will follow the same process as the election of EAs
  1. Due to the need for the people to know whether they are voting for an EA or a Scribe, the election of a Scribe will begin exactly one week after the election of the EAs concludes
  1. A Scribe shall have no voting power in the EA body, under any circumstance
  2. To ensure a Scribe has no influence, the Scribe may not talk on voting matters, outside of enforcing proper execution of Robert's Rules of Order, during EA meetings
  1. As outlined, all meetings will be voice recorded and transcribed to ensure rule I.B.4 is followed
  2. A Scribe is to detail in the minutes anytime he/she must speak and must recount what was said and spoken about
  1. If at any time a Scribe shall speak to enforce Robert's Rules, or for any other reason, they must annotate in the notes the exact time in the voice recording of the aforementioned occurrence  
  1. To ensure that the elected body is in office for ample time to properly serve the Captain Community, EAs will be voted in for a term of two years
  1. EAs will be allowed to serve no more than two terms total
  1. While an EA may serve two terms, these terms may not be consecutive.
  1. After the completion of an EAs first term, they must wait no less than one calendar year before being eligible to serve their second term
  2. The term limits, as detailed in I.C.1 - I.C.1.a.(1), along with this clause, shall henceforth be unamendable
  1. Should the event arise where there are NO eligible candidates for the EA body and/or an elected seat (Elected Administrators and the Scribe), a one-time extension may be granted to a previous EA
  1. As there would be no other candidates in any scenario that falls into I.C.1.a.(2).a, a formal election shall be bypassed in lieu of a vote to grant a one-time extension
  1. This vote requires a ¾ majority to approve, and will follow normal Community Voting procedure & rules
  1. If all willing and previous EAs/scribes have already been granted a one-time extension and a lack of eligible candidates still exists, The Captain Community may submit to amend I.D.1.(2).(a) once all other options have been exhausted
  1. Terms are decided by the seat being filled, not the individual. As a result, this means: should an EA come into office as the result of an emergency vote, their term limit will conclude at the two-year mark of the seat's original election, not two years after the emergency vote
  1. A sitting EA who is subject of a successful Recall Election by the Community loses all rights to hold office, and may not run again or sit on the elected body again, including the office of Elected Administrator & Scribe
  2. A sitting EA who steps down of their own accord may run for election again
  1. They must wait at least 1 full election cycle after stepping down to be eligible
  2. The EA seat they are vacating counts as an entire 2-year term regardless of the time served
  3. Should an EA attempt to step down to bypass rule I.D.1.b.(1), the sitting EAs may revoke their ability for future reelection with a 4 / 2 vote
  1. If a Recall Election has already been called, the EA may step down; however, the vote should continue as normal to determine the EAs eligibility for future office
  1. Should an EA come into office less than 6 months before the seat's re-election, the sitting EA for the said seat may run again in the next election
  1. The less-than-6 month period will not count towards their 2 term limit
  1. Prior to taking office, all voted-in candidates are required to give audio recording consent, which is addressed in Addendum III: Oath of Office
  2. Prior to taking office, all voted-in candidates must take place in a video interview with the rotating-out EA members, to verify age & sign Addendum III: Oath of Office
  1. To ensure that Captain has seasoned EAs at all time, and to give newly elected EAs the benefit of experienced peers to help acclimate them, all election terms will be staggered on a 4:3 split
  1. On odd-numbered years (2021, 2023, etc), 3 of the 7 seats, as well as the office of Scribe, will be up for election
  2. On even-numbered years (2022, 2024, etc.), 4 of the 7 seats will be up for election

                 C:2: In the founding year of Captain (2020), all 7 seats will be elected at once

  • To establish the aforementioned 4:3 split, the 4 EAs who receive the highest votes in the founding year of Captain will be elected for a full 2-year term
  • These 4 EAs will still be eligible for a 2nd term, but must still wait the 1 year period from the conclusion of their 1st term
  • The 3 EAs who receive the next most votes (behind the 4 highest) will be elected into office for a term of one year
  • This will allow the 4:3 split pattern on election years to be established
  • The 3 EAs with the lowest number of votes in the founding year of Captain (2020) will still be eligible to run for a 2nd term
  • They must still wait one year after the conclusion of their 1st term prior to running for their 2nd
  • Their first year as an EA will count as one full term when calculating their total max term limit of 2 terms

II. Candidate Selection & EA election

II. Candidate Selection & EA election

  1. Candidate selection will be an inclusive process designed to cultivate an atmosphere of open-minded Captain players from all corners of the format. There will be minimal (1) restriction as to who may submit their name for candidacy consideration by the community
  1. To ensure that the elected body of EAs and a Scribe is comprised of competent individuals, with the time and resources to dedicate to the position, candidates must be at least 18 years of age by the time they are in affirmed into office
  1. Lead Moderators, Moderators, and any Staff on the official Discord server are barred from holding any office
  2. Shall a Lead Moderator, Moderator, or Staff member choose to step down to run for EA, they must do so no less than 30 days prior to pre-candidacy selection
  3. Any individual who has access to sensitive information shall relinquish their ability to access such information, including but not limited to: Discord passwords, Facebook passwords, Twitter passwords, Reddit passwords, Captain Website passwords etc
  1. The Scribe should be the only elected person with access to the aforementioned entities
  1. Candidate selection will begin no more and no less than 45 days prior to election
  2. Candidate selection will end no more and no less than 15 days prior to election
  3. Any self-described member of the Captain Community may submit their name for consideration to be placed on the ballot
  1. A list of all pre-candidates will be published publicly and updated regularly through Captain's online platform by the expiring members of the elected body during the 30 day candidate selection period
  1. An expiring member of the elected body is defined as: a sitting Elected Administrator whose current term will end at the next election
  2. This is to prevent any corroboration of the non-expiring members with pre-candidates
  3. The list of pre-candidates is to be published through Captain's online platform alphabetically by the pre-candidates first name, and will include their social media "tag" they may go by
  1. The expiring EAs shall conduct a video conference with all pre-candidates to verify their age and identity before their name is placed online for consideration
  1. To prevent expired EA from forming a new persona to run again, a private document should be created to keep a list of all previous EAs names centralized
  1. No other information shall be stored other than legal name and a “checkbox” saying the pre-candidate is verified as being 18 years of age or older
  1. Pre-candidates must take all necessary precautions to ensure they deliver no personal information to the expiring EAs, aside from age and date of birth
  1. Pre-candidates are encouraged to have a print-off of their photo ID, with all unnecessary information blacked out, to present during this process
  1. The expiring EAs must take every precaution to avoid viewing or recording any personal information, aside from name and date of birth
  2. In the founding year of Captain, as there are no expiring EAs the process outlined in II.A.4.b shall instead be conducted by no less than 1 Lead Moderator of the Discord server with the addition of no less than 1 other staff member of the Discord Server
  1. All members of The Captain Community may "sign" a pre-candidates submission to show support for their inclusion on the ballot through Captain's online platform
  1. The "signing process" will open up no more and no less than 10 days prior to the election
  2. The "signing process" will close no more and no less than 3 days prior to the election to give the elected body time to prepare the final ballot
  3. Members of The Captain Community may sign no more than 3 pre-candidate’s submissions
  1. A pre-candidate's inclusion on the ballot will be decided by the number of signatures they obtained and will be measured by one of two methods. Should the candidate fall into either of the following two measures, they will be included on the election ballot:
  1. The pre-candidates who fall in the top 25% of pre-candidates with total signatures will be included
  2. The pre-candidates who obtained 10% of total signatures will be included

B.  Once candidates have been selected, as outlined in II.A, the chosen candidates will now move on to the final election

  1. An election for EA must begin no more than 15 days after the culmination of pre-candidacy selection
  1. In the founding year of Captain refer to II.A.3.a
  1. The vote to choose EA members must stay open for no more and no less than 7 calendar days
  2. The top candidates who received the highest number of votes, whose total number should be no more and no less than the total EA seats up for election, will become EA-Elects until the completion of the steps outlined in I.D.2 & I.D.3
  3. The EA-Elects must take their respective seats no more than 7 days after the completion of the election, or at the time of all signed Oaths of Office being posted, whichever is sooner
  1. Shall an EA-Elect fail to follow the steps outlined in I.C.2 & I.C.3, they will abdicate their position
  1. Should this happen, an emergency election should automatically trigger no more than 7 days after their deadline to meet the aforementioned criteria
  1. Should an EA-Elect fail to meet requirements in I.C.2 & I.C.3, they will abdicate their right to run for office for two rotation cycles
  1. An EA-Elect abdicating their right to hold office due to the provisions in II.B.4.a, the entire term they abdicated shall count towards their term limit, as outlined in I.C
  1. Members of The Captain Community may vote for no more than 3 candidates

C.  Whereas it is important to the integrity of this document, all EA-Elects must be sworn into office

  1. The swearing into office will consist of the signing of the Oath of Office, which will be attached to The Constitution as a separate document, and shall be voted on separately
  1. This process must be conducted by the expiring EAs who are rotating out
  2. As an additional step of transparency, the document shall be read out loud by one expiring EA member, as outlined in II.C.1.a and at least one non-expiring EA member must also be present
  1. The EA-elect shall repeat after the sitting EA, replacing any necessary instances of "your name" with the EA-Elect's first name and discord tag
  2. The process detailed in II.C.B must be recorded and must be published to the online platform, for community access, no more than 24 hours after signing of the Oath of Office
  1. Shall an EA-Elect refuse to sign this oath, refer to I.C.2 & I.C.3
  1. The EA-Elect shall immediately send a digital copy of the signed Oath of Office to both members of the EA who were present for the signing
  2. The signed Oath of Office must be uploaded to the online platform, for community viewing
  1. All signed Oaths of Office must be posted by the timeframe outlined in II.B.4
  2. All Oaths of Office must be uploaded at the same time
  3. All Oaths of Office must be uploaded no more than 7 days after the completion of the election, and no sooner than the last Oath of Office being signed, whichever is first
  1. Shall an Oath of Office not be signed, refer to section II.B.4
  2. Any unsigned Oaths shall not impede EA-Elects who have signed their Oath of Office
  1. The transition of power, as detailed in II.C.4, shall continue as normal for the EA-Elects who have signed
  2. Those who have not signed will have their seat sent to an emergency election, as outlined in II.B.4.a.1
  3. The current expiring EA who is rotating out will still rotate out per normal, as all EA powers are suspended as outlined in I.B.1
  1. The transition of office to EA-Elect will occur the moment the Oaths of Office are uploaded to the online platform, as outlined in II.C.3
  2. In the founding year of Captain, as there will be no expiring EAs, the process outlined in II.C.1.a will instead be conducted by no less than 1 Lead Moderator of the Discord server with the addition of no less than 1 staff member of the Discord server

III. Recall Votes

III. Recall Votes will exist as a way for the Community to maintain all power by removing EAs from office that are deemed incapable

  1. Recall elections can follow the normal voting process as any format change but are not limited to it
  1. The other 6 sitting EAs may vote to move forward to a public Recall Vote with a 4 / 2 majority
  2. To prevent power-grabs by a coalition of the EAs, the Captain Community will maintain the power to supersede their decision to not move forward to a Recall Vote
  1. Any member of the Captain Community may submit a Recall Vote request for any sitting EA member's through Captain's online platform
  1. Once a Recall Vote is submitted by a member of the community, it will be automatically published to all online & social media platforms for a public vote
  1. A Recall Vote will be open for no less than 5 days
  1. In the case of a community member-submitted Recall Vote, the vote will automatically be made open for exactly five days

3.   Submissions for a Recall Vote will use the same rolling average qualifier to move forward to vote, as outlined in IV.H.2 - IV.H.2.a

B.    A Recall Vote will be a success if it garners support from ⅔ majority of the community-made-votes

  1. ⅔ majority will be measured by the number of all votes made on the Recall Vote, not by total members of the community

C.    Multiple Recall Votes for multiple different EA members may be held at once, with no limit

  1. In the event that the community deems it necessary to remove the entire elected body, the process for establishing a new body with a 4:3 split will revert to the process outlined in I.C2

D.    A successful Recall Vote will call for the remaining EAs to hold an immediate emergency election

  1. Emergency Election process will follow as outlined in I.B.1

IV. Duties of the EA

IV. Duties of the EAs will purposely be designed to minimize their influence and power. While 'some' power will inevitably end up in the hands of the elected body, the system is designed to directly reduce it and allow for more of a "Pure Democracy" system to be established

  1. The elected body is designated as Elected Administrators to ensure the message is clear - they are administrators, paper-pushers, and community-servants in the truest sense
  2. Elected Administrators must remain active in the community and its different online presences
  1. Ultimately, the sole duty of the EAs is to actively listen to what the community is talking about, what they might wish to see happen to the format and help to guide and foster their ideas

 C.  EAs will meet on no less than a quarterly basis

  1. The current elected body may decide on their quarterly meeting day (IE, 1st Monday of the quarter, etc). This is to ensure that the current elected body can actively do what is best for the community by scheduling times that work for its members
  1. The EA should have a recurring set day & time, which can be amended should the sitting EAs come to an agreement
  1. The minutes of this conversation, as all minutes, should publically be posted
  1. Each EA meeting shall both be transcribed by a scribe and voice recorded
  1. Both the transcription and voice recording must be posted to the online platform no more than 48 hours after the conclusion of the meeting
  1. To ensure proper representation and deter the blocking of an EA member, all EA meetings should be scheduled for a time that is respectful of all time zones and mutually beneficial to all members
  2. No less than every 6 months, and at least twice per calendar year, the EA is required to have a Town Hall Meeting
  1. The Town Hall Meeting will follow Robert's Rules Of Order
  1. Robert's Rules should be enforced by EAs selected scribe, to help promote transparency
  1. The online platform should be amended to allow for any Captain Community member to submit questions and topics of discussion no less than 5 days prior to the Townhall Meeting
  1. All submissions are required to be publicly published for transparency
  1. The Town Hall Meeting should incorporate live discussion in the form of both voice and text options, to ensure proper representation
  2. Every sitting EA member is to be given no less than 5 minutes of open discussion, to ensure proper representation
  3. Each Town Hall Meeting should last for no less than 60 minutes, and no longer than 120 minutes, as decided by the sitting EA
  4. Town Hall Meetings should follow the same guidelines for EA meetings, as outlined in IV.C.1
  5. No less than 10 community members should be given the floor to speak on voice
  1. Each speaker should be given no less than 3 minutes to discuss, including questions
  1. EA members are required to reschedule all quarterly meetings that do not have the full 7 EAs presence
  1. This rule is superseded by the requirement of 1 quarterly meeting, as defined in IV.C
  1. All reasonable efforts should be taken to ensure all 8 members, including the scribe, are present; however, should this become impossible, the EA members should hold the meeting on a day/time that will yield the highest attendance of members
  1. A Recall Vote will automatically trigger should an EA member be unable to make more than 2 meetings in a row, or at least 3 in a calendar year
  1. Recalled EAs lose their eligibility to hold office again, as defined in I.D.1.b.(1)
  2. Should an EA find themself unable to meet the demands of the position due to health or personal life, they may voluntarily request an emergency election be held to temporarily replace their seat with an Interim EA
  1. Interim EA elections should follow the same procedure outlined in section II; however, the vote must happen prior to the next quarterly meeting
  2. Should an EA follow the process outlined in IV.G.2 - IV.G.2.b, they will not lose their rights to be EA; however, the time their seat was filled by an Interim EA will count towards their term
  1. The time an Interim EA spends in office cannot exceed 6 months
  1. Should the sitting EA be unable to return after 6 months, an emergency election should be held to find a permanent replacement. This will follow all rules and regulations outlined in I.B.1
  1. Should an EA find themself unable to meet the demands of the position for any reason, they may voluntarily vacate their seat
  1. Should this happen, an emergency election should be held to find a permanent replacement, as outlined in I.B.1.b
  1. During an EA meeting, EA members are required to vote on any community submission to move to a popular vote that gained enough community support
  1. All members of The Captain Community can submit a request through the online platform for the EA to vote on
  1. There should be an open and on-going submission option on online platforms
  1. EA members are required to vote on any submission that gained proper support (outlined in IV.H.2-IV.H.3) by a cutoff period of 48 hours prior to the commencement of the scheduled EA meeting
  1. A community-submitted proposal may reference only one (1) card and/or rule
  1. Any submission that references the changing/(un)banning of multiple cards or rules shall be immediately removed and disregarded
  1.  The EA is required to vote on any Community-submitted Proposal if It falls into the top 25% of currently active proposal
  1. 25% is calculated by looking at a rolling average of the total number of proposals submitted over the last 6 months
  2. Community-submitted proposals will be cleared out and start anew immediately after each quarterly EA meeting
  1. The EA is required to vote on no less than 10, but is not required to vote on more than 50, community submissions each quarter, if able.
  1. This measure (IV.H.3) supersedes IV.H.2 when applicable: If the 25% rolling average of submissions and/or total number of community submissions is less than 10, 10 must be voted on. If the 25% rolling average of submissions exceeds 50, the EA is not required to vote on more than 50
  1. All proposals will be reset at the time of the cutoff for submission for the EA meeting, as outlined in IV.H.1.b
  1. Any proposal that was reset as a result of IV.H.4 may, however, be resubmitted shall the Community deem it necessary
  1. Each EA member can submit any topic to be voted on to move to a public vote
  1. Should the EA vote on or submit a topic to Community Vote, which was not a required topic of vote, as outlined in IV.H.2, it shall not count towards their required quote, as outlined in IV.H.3
  1. EA members can vote with one of 3 options: Yes, No, or Abstained
  1. As there is a quorum of all 7 EAs being present for votes, an abstain will not be factored into the vote total for calculating majority and percentages
  1. All vote results for all topics must be recorded by the scribe and publicly posted to the online platforms for transparency
  2. Any topic to amend bans and unbans must move to a Community Vote if it receives 5 or more votes in favor of the change from EAs
  3. Any topic to amend format comprehensive rules must move to a community vote if it receives 6 or more votes
  4. Any topic to amend the Constitution or governance structure must move to a community vote vote if it receives 6 or 7 votes
  5. Any vote to move towards a Recall Election is outlined in III.A.1
  1. EAs are required to publish their meeting agenda to the official Captain website & Discord server no less than 48 hours prior to the meeting

1.  The agenda should list all proposals that gained enough support and are thus moving to vote by the EA

  1. This must include figures as to how much support each proposal earned

2.  The agenda should list all failed proposals that did not make it onto the voting list

  1. This must include figures as to how much support each submission received

J.  EAs are required to provide a brief (500-word max) recounting of each vote and why they voted the way they did

  1. This must be released along with the official results of all votes
  1. EAs may use discretion as to if this should be included with all results, or published in a separate document

V.  Community Votes

V.  Community Votes are to immediately follow any vote that the EA passes along, as outlined in section IV. Community Votes will be open to all self-described members of the Captain Community

  1. A Community Vote must occur no more than 3 calendar days after the EA has decided to pass a community submitted proposal onto a community vote

B.  A Community Vote must remain open for no less than 7 calendar days

C.  All public votes should be made available on all secure online platforms, and promoted on others, to ensure proper representation of the community's wishes

D.  To promote transparency, all voting numbers and data will be posted to the online platform for the general community to see

  1. Voting Data and information will not be released until the vote has concluded and the results are announced, to prevent it from causing voter bias
  2. This data is to be published no less than 3 days after the vote is over

E.  Once a Community Vote has concluded, should it have garnered enough support, it is to be implemented no more than 7 calendar days from the end of the vote

  1. Votes on format changes:
  1. A  ⅔ majority is required to ban/unban cards
  2. A 75% majority is required to amend format comprehensive rules of Captain
  1. Votes on governance structure, Constitutional changes, and amendments
  1. A 75% majority is required

H.      EA & scribe elections

  1. No majority is needed, the top candidates with the most votes will move on
  1. Recall Election:
  1. A ⅔ majority is required

VI.  Emergency Votes

VI.  Emergency Votes are held when the elected body falls under 7 persons

  1. All voting abilities of the EA is suspended, minus holding an emergency vote, until such time that the elected body is back at full capacity
  2. Emergency votes will follow the normal candidate voting structure, as outlined in I.B

VII. Addendum I:

Rules & Card Legality

Part 0 - What are the Rules?

Magic the Gathering is a very complicated game with over 25 years of cards, mechanics, and interactions. Despite this fact, you can teach someone how to play Magic in under 20 minutes. This is accomplished by having two sets of rules. The Basic Rules which are user friendly, and cover all of the really important information that a new player needs to know, and The Comprehensive Rules which comprise an ever growing 240+ page document meant to cover everything so that when a corner case comes up, the correct course of action can be taken.

As such Captain also has two sets of rules. The Simplified Rules and the Comprehensive Rules which are meant to be read as if they were a part of the Wizards of the Coasts Comprehensive Rules. By maintaining two sets of rules, the Captain format will both be easy for players to understand, and robust enough that a Magic Judge won’t have to make rulings on the fly to resolve unusual situations.

Part 1 - The Simplified Rules

The simplified rules were initially written by the Commander Rules Committee for Commander and were adapted by the IT team for Captain. They are meant to serve as the primary way that the Captain community interacts with the rules of Captain. That is to say that the majority of Rule Change proposals submitted by the community will directly affect the wording of The Simplified Rules. A separate archive document (found here) will detail how the rules have changed as the format has grown and evolved.

Deck Construction Rules

  1. Players choose a legendary creature as the Captain for their deck.
  2. A card’s colour identity is its color plus the colour of any mana symbols in the card’s rules text. A card’s color identity is established before the game begins, and cannot be changed by game effects. The cards in a deck may not have any colours in their color identity which are not in the colour identity of the deck’s Captain.
  3. A Captain deck must contain exactly 100 cards, including the Captain. If you’re playing a companion, it must adhere to color identity and singleton rules. While it is not part of the deck, it is effectively a 101st card.
  4. With the exception of basic lands, no two cards in the deck may have the same English name. Some cards (e.g. Relentless Rats) may have rules text that overrides this restriction.

Play Rules

  1. Players begin the game with 40 life.
  2. Each Captain begins the game in the Command Zone. While a Captain is in the command zone, it may be cast, subject to the normal timing restrictions for casting creatures. Its owner must pay  2  for each time it was previously cast from the command zone; this is an additional cost.
  3. If a Captain is in a graveyard or in exile and that card was put into that zone since the last time state-based actions were checked, its owner may put it into the command zone. If a Captain would be put into its owner’s hand or library from anywhere, its owner may put it into the command zone instead. This replacement effect may apply more than once to the same event.
  4. Being a Captain is not a characteristic [MTG CR109.3], it is a property of the card and tied directly to the physical card. As such, “Captain-ness” cannot be copied or overwritten by continuous effects. The card retains it’s Captain-ness through any status changes, and is still a Captain even when controlled by another player.
  5. If a player has been dealt 21 points of combat damage by a particular Captain during the game, that player loses a game.
  6. Captains are subject to the Legend rule; a player cannot control more than one legend with the same name.
  7. Abilities which bring other card(s) you own from outside the game into the game (such as Living Wish; Spawnsire of Ulamog; Karn, the Great Creator) do not function in Captain.

Part 2 - The Comprehensive Rules (or Captain’s Comprehensive Rules)

The Comprehensive Rules were written by SillyHalfOrc at the request of HobitWhoCould. Section 903 contains the rules for Commander. It is referenced numerous times throughout the comprehensive rules and is the ultimate authority on Commander and Brawl. As such the starting point for Captain’s Comprehensive Rules is 903.13 ( the next open subsection of 903). This is because Captain is effectively a Commander Variant at the time of writing.

The goal of The Comprehensive Rules is to make everything in the Simplified Rules functionally true for players of the Captain community. As such it is intended that direct community editing of the Comprehensive rules will be rare. Instead whenever the Simplified Rules are updated by the community, Individuals familiar with Magic’s Comprehensive Rules will update Captain’s Comprehensive rules to make the update to the simplified rules “official.” In the event a member of the Captain community notices an error in The Comprehensive Rules, they can submit a proposal to the Elected Administrator’s like with any other rules change. Such changes are rare and will likely not reach a community vote as it is expected that such issues will be approved (or possibly soundly refuted) by the same individuals who update The Comprehensive Rules.

Part 5 - FAQ

The Comprehensive rules don’t define what a Captain is, why?

Commander as a property of a card is well defined in Magic’s rules. While a rule could be added that replaces the word “Commander” with “Captain”, it is largely unnecessary for the format. It also runs the risk of breaking something once you get deeper into the rules.

Why don’t the rules refer to the banlist?

Magic keeps its rules and ban list separate to reduce the number of rules updates that need to happen. It makes sense to do the same.

Part 6 - Comprehensive Rules

908. Captain

  • 908.1. In the Captain variant, each deck is led by a legendary creature designated as that deck’s Captain. The Captain format is a format that is managed and curratited by its community MTGCaptain.cards. The Captain variant uses all the normal rules for a Magic game, with the following additions.
  • 908.2. A Captain game may be a two-player game or a multiplayer game. The default multiplayer setup is the Free-for-All variant with the attack multiple players option and without the limited range of influence option. See rule 806, “Free-for-All Variant.”
  • 908.3. Each deck has a legendary creature card designated as its Captain. This designation is not a characteristic of the object represented by the card; rather, it is an attribute of the card itself. The card retains this designation even when it changes zones.
    Example: A Captain that’s been turned face down (due to Ixidron’s effect, for example) is still a Captain. A Captain that’s copying another card (due to Cytoshape’s effect, for example) is still a Captain. A permanent that’s copying a Captain (such as a Body Double, for example, copying a Captain in a player’s graveyard) is not a Captain.
  • 908.3a Some planeswalker cards have an ability that states the card can be your Commander. Such planeswalker cards can be your Captain. This ability modifies the rules for deck construction, and it functions before the game begins. See rule 113.6m.
  • 908.3b If a player’s Captain is a meld card and it’s melded with the other member of its meld pair, the resulting melded permanent is that player’s Captain.
  • 908.3c If a player’s Captain is a component of a merged permanent, the resulting merged permanent is that player’s Captain.
  • 908.3d If an effect refers to controlling a Commander, it refers to a permanent on the battlefield that is a Captain. If an effect refers to casting a Captain, it refers to a spell that is a Captain. If an effect refers to a Commander in a specific zone, it refers to a card in that zone that is a Captain.
  • 908.4. The Captain variant uses color identity to determine what cards can be in a deck with a certain Captain. The color identity of a card is the color or colors of any mana symbols in that card’s mana cost or rules text, plus any colors defined by its characteristic-defining abilities (see rule 604.3) or color indicator (see rule 204).
    Example: Bosh, Iron Golem is a legendary artifact creature with mana cost {8} and the ability “{3}{R}, Sacrifice an artifact: Bosh, Iron Golem deals damage equal to the sacrificed artifact’s converted mana cost to any target.” Bosh’s color identity is red.
  • 908.4a Color identity is established before the game begins.
  • 908.4b Reminder text is ignored when determining a card’s color identity. See rule 207.2.
  • 908.4c The back face of a double-faced card (see rule 711) is included when determining a card’s color identity. This is an exception to rule 711.4a.
    Example: Civilized Scholar is the front face of a double-faced card with mana cost {2}{U}. Homicidal Brute is the back face of that double-faced card and has a red color indicator. The card’s color identity is blue and red.
  • 908.5. Each Captain deck is subject to the following deck construction rules.
  • 908.5a Each deck must contain exactly 100 cards, including its Captain. In other words, the minimum deck size and the maximum deck size are both 100.
  • 908.5b Other than basic lands, each card in a Captain deck must have a different English name.
  • 908.5c A card can be included in a Captain deck only if every color in its color identity is also found in the color identity of the deck’s Captain.
    Example: Wort, the Raidmother is a legendary creature with mana cost {4}{R/G}{R/G}. Wort’s color identity is red and green. Each card in a Wort Captain deck must be only red, only green, both red and green, or have no color. Each mana symbol in the mana cost or rules text of a card in this deck must be only red, only green, both red and green, or have no color.
  • 908.5d A card with a basic land type may be included in a Captain deck only if each color of mana it could produce is included in the Captain’s color identity.
    Example: Wort, the Raidmother’s color identity is red and green. A Wort Captain deck may include land cards with the basic land types Mountain and/or Forest. It can’t include any land cards with the basic land types Plains, Island, or Swamp.
  • 908.6. At the start of the game, each player puts their Captain from their deck face up into the Command Zone. Then each player shuffles the remaining 99 cards of their deck so that the cards are in a random order. Those cards become the player’s library.
  • 908.7. Once the starting player has been determined, each player sets their life total to 40 and draws a hand of seven cards. (Command Zone shouldn’t change as it is a supported zone in magic. For example, emblems go in the Command Zone)
  • 908.8. A player may cast a Captain they own from the Command Zone. A Captain cast from the Command Zone costs an additional {2} for each previous time the player casting it has cast it from the Command Zone that game. This additional cost is informally known as the “Commander Tax.” (Commander Tax might not be worth changing similar to Command Zone)
  • 908.9. A Captain may return to the Command Zone during a Captain game.
  • 908.9a If a Captain is in a graveyard or in exile and that card was put into that zone since the last time state-based actions were checked, its owner may put it into the Command Zone. This is a state-based action. See rule 704.
  • 908.9b If a Captain would be put into its owner’s hand or library from anywhere, its owner may put it into the Command Zone instead. This replacement effect may apply more than once to the same event. This is an exception to rule 614.5.
  • 908.9c If a Captain is a melded permanent or a merged permanent and its owner chooses to put it into the Command Zone using the replacement effect described in rule 903.9b, that permanent and each component representing it that isn’t a Captain are put into the appropriate zone, and the card that represents it and is a Captain is put into the Command Zone.
  • 908.10. The Captain variant includes the following specification for winning and losing the game. All other rules for ending the game also apply. (See rule 104.)
  • 908.10a A player that’s been dealt 21 or more combat damage by the same Captain over the course of the game loses the game. (This is a state-based action. See rule 704.)
  • 908.11. If a player is allowed to bring a card from outside the game into a Captain game, that player can’t bring a card into the game this way if it has the same name as a card that player had in their starting deck, if it has the same name as a card that the player has already brought into the game, or if any color in its color identity isn’t in the color identity of the player’s Captain.

Addendum II: Motion to Determine How to Handle Communications with WOTC

Addendum II:

Motion to Determine How to Handle Communications with WOTC

Proposed October 11, 2020

WHEREAS, WOTC may reach out to the captain community at some point in the indefinite future, and;

WHEREAS, such a line of communication and engagement may be conducive to furthering relations with WOTC and the greater MTG community, and;

WHEREAS, it is important to ensure that the EAs are an independent body free from outside influence, and;

WHEREAS, the nature of how to handle such communications is a sensitive and contentious topic, and may have legal ramifications;

BE IT THEREFORE RESOLVED, THAT the 7 voting members of the EAs be subject to the following:

  1. They shall have no backdoor or closed door communications with WOTC representatives.
  2. They shall have no backdoor or closed door communications with any third-party organization related to MTG that is not a strictly personal, professional (in regards to a professional job or occupation), or legal matter.
  3. They shall not issue public statements to such third-party organizations on behalf of the community on any given topic without first seeking community approval.
  4. The Scribe shall act as the official liaison between the EAs, any third-party organization, and the community as a whole, but may not make or issue public statements on the topic without community approval.
  5. This policy is amendable at a later date, pending a community vote.

Addendum IV: Founding Year Pre-Candidacy & Candidate Elections

Addendum IV:

Founding Year Pre-Candidacy & Candidate Elections

WHEREAS, in the founding year of Captain, the Community and its memberbase is still in infancy;

WHEREAS, it is fundamentally important to the integrity of Captain to formally establish its elected body in a timely manner;

WHEREAS, the timeframe(s) outlined throughout section II of The Constitution of The Captain Format© would put undue burden on The Captain Community;

BE IT THEREFORE RESOLVED, THAT the timeframe for voting in the founding year of Captain (2020) and its first set of elections, as outlined in section II of The Constitution of The Captain Format©, will be replaced on a one time basis with the following:

  1. The process of pre-candidacy selection will be removed
  2. Any member of The Captain Community will be given exactly 7 days to submit their name for election
  3. At the end of the 7 day period, barring any time restraints by IT to upload the names into the voting system, each and every candidate who submitted their name will be put up for formal election
  4. All candidates names should be placed alphabetically, using their Discord Tag
  5. Once the candidates are put into the system and uploaded, voting should immediately commence
  6. There will be a 7 day voting period
  7. EA and Scribe elections will happen at the same time
  8. Members of The Captain Community my cast their vote for up to 3 candidates or for 25% of total candidates, whichever is greater
  9. The top 7 EA candidates and the top 1 Scribe candidate (based on number of votes) will be elected
  10.  At the completion of the vote and the swearing in of the EA & Scribe, this addendum will lose all authority, and all subsequent votes should follow section II of The Constitution of The Captain Format ©
